Research Participation Notice

Please read carefully before creating an account — NeuroSpark Brain Training Study

🔬 ISEF 2025–2026 Human Participant Guidelines
🎯 Purpose of This Study

This study investigates how brain-training games affect cognitive performance (memory, attention, processing speed, and logic) in high school students. Your gameplay data will be analyzed to identify patterns in cognitive skill development. This study follows the ISEF 2025–2026 Human Participant Research Guidelines.

🎮 What You Will Be Asked to Do

You will play brain-training mini-games on this platform (Memory Match, Sequence, Speed Find, and more). The app automatically records your scores, response times, and accuracy during each session. You may be asked to play across multiple sessions.

⏱️ Time Required

Each game session takes approximately 2–5 minutes. Total study participation may involve multiple sessions over several days or weeks, as directed by the researcher.

⚠️ Potential Risks

There are no known physical or psychological risks associated with this study. The games are non-stressful and designed for general audiences. You may stop at any time if you feel uncomfortable.

🔒 Anonymity & Confidentiality

Your privacy is protected in the following ways:

• Your name and Participant ID will never appear in any published report, presentation, or display.
• Only anonymous, aggregated data (e.g., average scores across participants) will be reported.
• Raw data is accessible only to the researcher and is stored securely.
• No data will be shared with third parties.
🙋 Voluntary Participation

Participation in this study is completely voluntary. You may choose not to participate, stop at any time, or skip any question or game session without any negative consequence. Choosing not to participate will not affect your standing in any class or activity.

📞 Contact & Questions

If you have any questions about this study, please contact the student researcher or their adult supervisor before proceeding. You have the right to ask questions at any time during the study.
